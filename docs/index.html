01 — The Problem

Everything else is either too big or too bare.

Enterprise telemetry stacks are engineering projects in themselves. Simple collectors give you raw data and nothing more.

Enterprise Platforms
// What you sign up for
router
  → Kafka
  → ClickHouse
  → dashboards

Powerful analytics. Real insights. Also: a Kafka cluster, ClickHouse replication, and a team to maintain it.

Low-Level Collectors
// What you actually get
router
  → raw flows
  → ...now what?

Captures flows. Forwards them. No analytics, no anomaly detection, no integration. You build the rest.

There's no widely adopted solution that gives you single-node deployment, built-in flow analytics, and simple Prometheus integration with low resource requirements.

  • 01
    Ingestion Flodar binds a UDP socket and receives flow exports from any NetFlow v5/v9, IPFIX, or sFlow-capable device. No agent installation on your routers.
  • 02
    Normalization All protocols decode into a single FlowRecord model — source/dest IPs, ports, protocol, packets, bytes, TCP flags. One format, all protocols.
  • 03
    Sliding Window Analytics Traffic statistics computed over 10s, 1m, and 5m windows: flows/sec, bytes/sec, top talkers, protocol distribution, unique source IPs.
  • 04
    Explainable Detection UDP flood, SYN flood, port scan, and destination hotspot rules. Every alert lists its indicators — packet rates, source counts, traffic ratios — so you understand what triggered it.
  • 05
    Prometheus + Grafana Metrics exposed at /metrics. Plug a pre-built Grafana dashboard in and you have real-time network visibility in minutes.

04 — Detection

Alerts that explain themselves.

Every Flodar alert carries the evidence. You see what triggered it, not just that something triggered.

Alert: possible_udp_flood

Target: 192.168.1.10
Window: 10 seconds

Indicators:
  - packets/sec exceeded baseline by 12×
  - 3,400 unique source IPs observed
  - 92% UDP traffic
Volumetric

UDP floods, SYN floods — detected via packet rate spikes, protocol ratios, and abnormal source IP counts.

Behavioral

Port scans and destination hotspots — single sources contacting many ports, or disproportionate traffic to one destination.
